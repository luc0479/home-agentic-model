How a business need becomes maverick spend

Sarah needs endpoint security software for fifty laptops. She knows what she wants. She has a quote. What she doesn't have is any idea how procurement works. Three emails later — all to the wrong people — she's told her preferred vendor isn't on the approved list and the queue is six to eight weeks. So she raises the PO direct. One more maverick transaction. Nobody benchmarks it.

Her week, honestly
Three emails to find the right procurement contact
Preferred vendor not on the approved supplier list
Six-to-eight week sourcing queue for a £20k purchase
PO raised direct to preferred vendor — maverick spend no-one tracks
Corporate card for anything under five thousand — every time
What she tells us
"By the time the business comes to me, they've already chosen the supplier."
— Category Manager, Group Functions

Instead it's defined by duplicated onboarding, contracts signed four months after they expire, and quarterly reviews that feel like audits — not conversations.

What she experiences
Six weeks to complete onboarding — same forms, same checks, run twice across entities
Contract signed four months after expiry — work continued on a lapsed agreement
QBRs built from Centrica's own invoice data — no forward agenda, no strategic conversation
Three percent volume rebate agreed eighteen months ago — never tracked, never claimed
Separate supplier IDs across Group, CES, PH Jones — one company, four records
